Chapter 6 - TECHNICAL GUIDELINES FOR THE IMPLEMENTATION OF STUDENT REGULATIONS

Tardiness During Onsite Learning

1st-3rd Late

Students must report to the teacher on duty to complete an admission slip before entering class.

Students are strictly limited to a maximum of three late arrivals.

4th Late

On the fourth instance of tardiness, the homeroom teacher issues a verbal warning.

5th Late

On the fifth instance, parents receive a written reminder by text message from the Vice-Principal for Student Affairs, and the student must submit a written pledge not to be late again.

6th Late

On the sixth instance, the student receives Warning Letter I (SP I) and an “Satisfactory (S)” grade in the General Behavior - Discipline div of the report slip.

  1. Absence due to illness – onsite and remote learning

    Parents must notify the homeroom teacher on the same day, sending a photo of the doctor’s note via WhatsApp, if the student is absent due to illness (onsite or remote).

    For onsite learning, a medical certificate must be submitted within three days of returning to school if the illness lasts more than one day.

    Failure to submit the certificate, or absence without formal parental notice during onsite/remote learning, is recorded as unexplained absence (alpha).

  2. Absence for special reasons – onsite learning

    Students must submit a written request to the Principal at least one week in advance.

    The Principal will comment on any request for a make-up assessment.

  3. Absence without explanation – onsite and remote learning

    Any absence without explanation is recorded as unexplained absence.

    Unexplained absences reduce the participation grade.

    Five consecutive unexplained absences constitute withdrawal from school.

    A 5 % deduction is applied to the subject grade on the day the total unexplained absences reach five.

Leaving class for internal matters – onsite learning

Students must use a hall pass to visit the restroom or refill water bottles.

An off-classroom form is required for meetings, counselling, or fellowship practice.

Not using the pass or form is treated as leaving class without permission.

Leaving class for external matters – onsite learning

Students must submit a permission note to the homeroom teacher.

They then obtain an off-campus form from the Vice-Principal for Student Affairs with the parents’ letter attached.

The completed form is given to the security officer before leaving, and the parent/guardian signs it on collection.

Security staff will ask the student’s pick-up person to sign the off-campus form.

Leaving class due to illness – onsite learning

A seriously ill student may go home early after parental confirmation of the authorized pick-up person.

The student obtains an off-campus form from the Vice-Principal for Student Affairs and submits it to security for signature.

The completed form is given to the security officer before leaving, and the parent/guardian signs it on collection.

Security staff will ask the student’s pick-up person to sign the off-campus form.

Leaving class without permission – onsite learning

Student who leaves class without permission receives a verbal warning and the incident is recorded.

After four such incidents,the student must submit a written statement.

Students will receive a First Warning Letter (SP I) if a similar violation occurs in the future.

Catch-up assessments – onsite learning

Students have the right to take a make-up test with full score if they are absent due to: (a) illness(proven by a doctor’s note), (b) bereavement due to a death in the family, or (c) participation in a competition assigned by the school. For points(a) and (b),students must submit a permission letter to their homeroom teacher no later than 3 days after returning to school.

Students may request to take a make-up test if they are participating in an exam or competition on a personal basis, by submitting an official participation letter no later than 1 week in advance.

Requests to take a make-up test due to other reasons are subject to the principal’s discretion. The maximum score for the make-up test will be the passing grade (KKM) if the request does not meet the conditions stated in points one and two, and is not considered an urgent matter.

For both onsite and distance learning, students will take the make-up test after all learning activities are completed (after school), according to the schedule set by the subject teacher.

Students who fail to attend the make-up test at the scheduled time will not be given a rescheduled session.

School does not provide shuttle services for make-up tests.

Remedial assessments

Students who have not met the Minimum Passing Criteria (KKM) have the right to take a remedial session, scheduled (afterschool/ after the learning process is completed) by the subject teacher.

Students who are unable to attend the remedial session due to certain reasons may take a remedial make-up, following the same provisions as the make-up test policy.

Students who fail to attend the remedial session at the scheduled time will not be given a rescheduled session.

The maximum remedial score equals the passing grade.

The school does not provide shuttle services for remedial tests.

Use of mobile phones / electronic devices – onsite learning

Students are allowed to bring mobile phones to school; however, they are not permitted to turn them on or use them during school hours, except with the subject teacher’s approval to support the learning topic.

Students are required to turn off and submit their mobile phones to their respective homeroom teachers before starting the morning devotion.

Students must submit their mobile phones to their Homeroom (HR) teacher's designated box during devotion

If a phone is needed for a lesson, students may retrieve it from their HR teacher but must return it to the box immediately after the class.

Phones are not permitted to be kept in bags or anywhere else in the classroom.

Students who misuse the policy on mobile phone or other electronic device usage will receive a consequence from the school in the form of a red violation slip.

If a violation occurs, the student will receive a red violation slip and a consequence in the form of their mobile phone or other electronic device being confiscated by the teacher and returned to the parents after 3 days by the Vice Principal for Student Affairs.

If a similar violation occurs in the future, the student will receive a red violation slip and a consequence in the form of their mobile phone or other electronic device being confiscated by the teacher and returned to the parents maximally on report card distribution day by the Vice Principal for Student Affairs.

Delivery of items to students – onsite learning

  • Students are only allowed to contact their parents or guardians to request the delivery of lunch and/or medication.
  • Lunch and/or medication sent from home must be labeled with the student’s full name and class.
  • Students must collect delivered items from the designated rack in the canteen.

Classlockers Usage

Each student is assigned one locker outside the home-base classroom.

Each student who wishes to use a locker is required to submit a deposit of Rp. 50,000 (fifty thousand rupiah) to their respective homeroom teacher.

The deposit is refunded at year-end if the key is returned in good condition.

However, students who fail to return the locker key at the end of the academic year in good condition or have lost it will not have their deposit money refunded.

During the academic year, if a student needs to duplicate their locker key, the homeroom teacher will lend the master key. The cost of duplication will be borne by the student.
